What is a Harmonograph?

A harmonograph is a mechanical device that uses pendulums to draw geometric figures. As the pendulums swing in different directions and at different frequencies, the pen traces out Lissajous curves — intricate, mathematically beautiful shapes ideal for pen plotter art.

This app simulates a rotary harmonograph with two pairs of oscillating rotors per pass, giving you precise control over shape, symmetry, and drawing style across multiple independent layers.

Tip — Sharing

The URL updates live as you work. ⬡ Share copies it to clipboard — anyone with the link opens the exact same drawing.

Paper & Margin

Sets output paper size, margin, and background color. The Seed controls oscillator starting positions — small changes can produce dramatically different drawings.

Canvas interaction

Click a pass card header to select it. A bounding box appears on the canvas — drag corners to scale, the circle above to rotate (snaps to 45°), inside to move. A pixel preview shows during drag; the full render commits on release.

▾ PEN Color · Line weight · Opacity · Density
ControlWhat it does
ColorInk color for this pass. Click the swatch to open the color picker.
Line weightStroke width in mm. Use the pen picker to match a real Micron or Staedtler pen size.
Opacity0–100%. Layering passes at lower opacity creates depth and optical blending.
DensityHow densely the curve is drawn. 1–100 logarithmic scale — most interesting range is 1–50. Higher values produce dense hatching.
▾ SHAPE Ratio pad · Detune X/Y · Amplitude

Controls the mathematical curve using the XY ratio pad. Drag to choose frequency ratios for X and Y oscillators. Snaps to ratio intersections with magnetic pull, floats freely between for detuned shapes.

Amplitude

The two rotors on each axis share their amplitude — drag the split bar to balance them. Left segment is R1 (X) or R3 (Y), right segment is R2 (X) or R4 (Y).

Let's Get Weird...

Let's get weird...Randomises all unlocked parameters

Triggers a slot-machine animation that randomises all unlocked parameters across all passes. Use the Lock toggles on each pass card to protect specific values — lock color to keep your palette while randomising shapes.

Export for Plotting

Click ↓ SVG to export. The SVG uses real-world millimetre coordinates matching the selected paper size — loads at the correct scale in Inkscape, LightBurn, or your plotter software.

Tip

Set line weight to match your actual pen (e.g. Micron 03 = 0.35mm) before exporting. This determines how the SVG stroke width is written.
